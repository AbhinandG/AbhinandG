A Comrehensive ElectroCardioGram (ECG) Feature Approach for the Recognition of Cardiovascular Abnormality

[code]

This project proposes novel feature extraction strategies of ElectroCardioGram (ECG) signals using Autoencoders. After experimenting with Stanalone Autoencoders (Vanilla, Variational, Recurrent) and Sequential Autoencoders (The output from a Vanilla Autoencoder being fed into a Recurrent Autoencoder), the best performing feature extraction approach was the one that used a hybrid latent feature map from a Vanilla Autoencoder and an LSTM Autoencoder. This autoencoder was also used to encode the Dynamic Time Warping (DTW) features, which were then used for binary classification.
A Multi-Modal PhonoCardioGram(PCG) and ElectroCardioGram(ECG) Approach in Cardiovascular Abnormality Recognition

Preliminary findings accepted and presented at ICCCSP 2023 [slides] [yet_to_be_published]

A new approach that combines PCG and ECG features has shown outstanding performance, outperforming traditional methods and achieving high accuracy, sensitivity, and specificity scores. Another form of multi-modality has also been explored, where the signal's wave-based features are combined with its image-based features when converted to its spectrogram.
An Efficient Stacking Ensemble Technique for Success Prediction of Indian Ventures

Accepted and Presented at ICDSAAI 2022 [IEEE]

A project that aims to use machine learning techniques to identify factors that could impact the success of startups. The project scraped data from various websites to create a dataset describing unicorn and non-unicorn startups, then applied several machine learning classifiers to predict which companies are likely to succeed. The project evaluated the models using various parameters and found that the stacked ensemble model had the highest accuracy of 80.1%. The model was then used to predict the success rate of startups featured in the reality show, Shark Tank India.
